## Three re-entry triggers (explained in full 2026-06-19)

Add capital to SEC0 only when at least ONE is confirmed:

### Trigger 1 — Price pullback ≥25% from recent high
- **Target:** SEC0/SEMI.AS ≤ €17.54 (range €17–18)
- **Why:** After a +98% YTD run, a 20–30% correction is historically normal and doesn't break the thesis. Same ETF, 25% cheaper starting point = significantly better long-term returns.
- **Analogy:** Street market sneaker seller who kept raising prices all year — eventually profit-takers flood the market and push prices back down. That's your entry.

### Trigger 2 — VIX spike above 25
- **Current VIX:** 16.4. Watch for spike above 25.
- **Why:** Fear above VIX 25 causes leveraged investors to face margin calls — forced to sell even if they believe in the investment. This pushes prices below fair value. Patient cash buyers benefit from others' panic.
- **Analogy:** Investors who borrowed money to buy are like someone with a bank loan on sneakers. When value drops below collateral threshold, they must sell at any price — creating a temporary discount.

### Trigger 3 — New base formation (VCP pattern)
- **What to look for:** SEC0 trades sideways for ≥5 consecutive weeks with daily price swings getting progressively smaller.
- **Why:** Confirms sellers are exhausted. Institutional buyers (large funds) are quietly accumulating. Safe to enter before the next breakout rather than chasing after it.
- **Analogy:** Pendulum swings getting smaller — sellers running out of energy before the next directional move.

### Decision tree
```
Is SEC0 down ≥25% from high (≤€17.54)?
├─ YES → Is VIX > 25 OR is a 5-week base forming?
│    ├─ BOTH → Strong entry signal
│    └─ ONE  → Good entry signal
└─ NO  → Has VIX spiked above 25?
     ├─ YES → Evaluate case by case (forced selling dip)
     └─ NO  → Is a 5-week base forming at current price?
          ├─ YES → Valid only if semis have meaningfully cooled from peak
          └─ NO  → WAIT. No trigger confirmed.
```

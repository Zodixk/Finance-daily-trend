## 10. Glossary

- **VIX:** the "fear index" — measures how much volatility (price swings) options traders expect in the S&P 500 over the next 30 days. Higher = more fear/uncertainty.
- **Uptrend participation:** the percentage of ~2,800 tracked US stocks currently in a confirmed uptrend. A low number means only a few stocks are driving the market up (narrow, fragile rally); a high number means the rally is broad and healthier.
- **Distribution day:** a day where a major index falls on higher trading volume than the prior day — a sign that large institutional investors are selling. Several of these clustered together is a warning sign.
- **Sector rotation:** the shift of investor money from one group of stocks (e.g., Technology) into another (e.g., Healthcare, Energy). "Defensive tilt" means money is moving into safer, less economically-sensitive sectors.
- **Cycle phase (Early/Mid/Late/Recession):** an estimate of where the economy sits in its boom-bust cycle, based on which sectors are leading. "Late cycle" suggests the current growth phase may be maturing.
- **Exposure ceiling:** the maximum percentage of an all-equities portfolio that current conditions justify holding. It is not a literal instruction to sell down to that number — it's a risk-budget guide for new money.
- **Stop loss:** a pre-set price level at which you plan to sell a losing position to cap further losses.
- **Composite confidence:** how much weight to put on today's overall read, given how much of the underlying data actually loaded successfully.
- **Follow-Through Day (FTD):** a specific high-volume rally day (day 4-7 of a rally attempt) that historically confirms a new market uptrend has started. None has occurred in the current rally attempt.

## 10. Glossary

- **VIX:** the "fear index" — measures how much volatility options traders expect in the S&P 500. Below 20 = calm markets.
- **Breadth score:** a 0-100 measure of how many stocks (not just the big ones) are participating in a market rally. Higher = healthier, broader rally.
- **Uptrend participation:** the percentage of ~2,800 tracked US stocks currently in a confirmed uptrend.
- **Distribution day:** a day where a major index falls on higher volume than the day before — a sign of large investors selling. Many of these clustered together can signal institutional selling pressure.
- **Composite confidence:** how much this briefing's overall conclusion should be trusted, based on how much of the underlying data actually loaded successfully today.
- **Exposure posture (ALLOW/RESTRICT/CASH-PRIORITY):** ALLOW = normal buying is fine; RESTRICT = pause new buying, hold what you have; CASH-PRIORITY = consider raising cash, market conditions are risky.
- **Stop loss:** the price at which you sell a position to limit losses if it keeps falling.
- **52-week high:** the highest price a stock/ETF has traded at over the past year.
- **PCE:** Personal Consumption Expenditures — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge.
- **NFP (Nonfarm Payrolls):** monthly US jobs report; a key signal for Fed policy and overall economic health.
- **ISM Manufacturing PMI:** a survey-based gauge of US factory activity; above 50 = expansion, below 50 = contraction.
- **FOMC:** the Federal Reserve committee that sets US interest rates.
- **Basis points (bps):** 1 bp = 0.01%; used for describing small interest rate moves.
- **EUR/USD context:** today's US index and price figures are in USD; SEC0.DE and VWCE.DE trade in EUR on Xetra, so no conversion is needed for your portfolio figures above.

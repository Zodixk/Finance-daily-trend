## 11. Glossary

Every technical term used today, explained in plain English:

- **VIX:** The "fear index." Measures how much volatility investors expect in the next 30 days. 17 = calm, >25 = elevated fear, >35 = crisis mode.
- **FOMC:** Federal Open Market Committee — the group inside the Fed that decides US interest rates. Met June 16-17. Next meeting: late July 2026.
- **Hawkish:** When the Fed signals that rates may rise. Bad for growth stocks because higher rates reduce the present value of future earnings. Opposite = "dovish."
- **Dot plot:** A chart showing each of the 18 Fed officials' anonymous forecast for where interest rates will be at year-end. "Raised dot plot" = officials now expect higher rates than before.
- **2-year yield:** The return on a 2-year US government bond. When it surges (+16bps in one day), markets expect the Fed to keep rates high — or raise them. Growth stocks (like PIE AI) fall when this rises.
- **Basis point (bp):** 1/100th of 1%. +16bps = +0.16%.
- **52w High/Low:** The highest/lowest price a stock reached in the past 52 weeks.
- **All-time high (ATH):** The highest price a stock has ever reached since listing.
- **Uptrend participation:** Percentage of stocks in an uptrend across all US sectors. 24.9% = only 1 in 4 stocks is trending up — a narrow, weak market.
- **Breadth score:** How many stocks are participating in the market's move. Low breadth = the index is being held up by a few big names while most stocks fall.
- **Sector rotation:** When investors shift money from one sector to another. Today: out of tech → into industrials and healthcare.
- **Stop loss:** A price at which you sell a position automatically to cap losses. Alessandro uses 7-8%. META's 8% stop from yesterday's close = ~$552.
- **Bullish / Bearish:** Bullish = expecting prices to rise. Bearish = expecting prices to fall.
- **Yield:** The return (income) a bond pays. Higher yield = higher borrowing cost for companies = lower stock valuations, especially for growth stocks.
- **CASH-PRIORITY:** Posture meaning: no new buys, protect existing positions, hold cash. Focus on preservation, not growth.
- **RESTRICT:** One step below CASH-PRIORITY. Selective new entries only at the best setups, with smaller position sizes.
- **ALLOW:** Full posture. New positions allowed under normal rules (max 10% each, 7-8% stop).
- **DCA (Dollar-Cost Averaging):** Buying a fixed euro amount at regular intervals regardless of price. What Alessandro does with VWCE.
- **EUV lithography:** The technology ASML monopolizes — uses extreme ultraviolet light to print tiny circuits inside advanced chips. No other company can do this at scale.
- **Custom AI accelerators:** Chips designed specifically for one company's AI workload (e.g., Google's TPU, Meta's MTIA). AVGO builds these for multiple hyperscalers.
- **Strait of Hormuz:** Narrow waterway between Iran and Oman through which ~20% of global oil passes. The US-Iran deal reopens it to commercial shipping.
- **CME FedWatch:** A tool showing the market's implied probability of Fed rate changes, derived from interest rate futures contracts.
- **Composite confidence:** A weighted average score (0-100%) across all skill outputs that measures how reliable today's briefing assessment is. 62% = MEDIUM — act with appropriate caution.

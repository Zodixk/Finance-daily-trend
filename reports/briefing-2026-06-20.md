## 11. Glossary

- **VIX:** The "fear index" — measures how much volatility traders expect in the S&P 500 over the next 30 days. Below 20 = calm markets. Above 30 = high fear.
- **Uptrend participation:** The percentage of stocks (out of ~2,800 tracked) that are currently in an uptrend. Low participation (below 30%) means the rally is narrow and fragile — only a few big stocks are driving the index higher.
- **Composite score (62.6/100):** A combined health score for the market, blending breadth, sector participation, momentum, and historical context. Below 60 = cautious; above 70 = healthy.
- **Macro regime — Broadening:** A market phase where leadership expands beyond the top mega-cap stocks. This is generally positive (more stocks participating), but if the transition fails, it can flip back to concentration or begin a bear phase.
- **Transition probability 70-90%:** The model estimates there is a 70-90% chance the current Broadening regime is shifting into a new phase. This is an "inflection zone" — the market is at a crossroads.
- **FOMC hawkish hold:** The Federal Reserve kept interest rates unchanged but signaled it is more likely to raise rates than cut. "Hawkish" = pro-rate-hike. This is generally negative for growth stocks and ETFs like SEC0 and VWCE.
- **Dot plot:** The Fed's internal forecast showing where each official expects rates to go. The dot plot "flipped" means officials now lean toward a hike rather than a cut in 2026.
- **PCE inflation:** Personal Consumption Expenditures — the Fed's preferred measure of inflation. Higher PCE = more pressure on the Fed to raise rates.
- **Distribution day:** A day when a major index falls on higher-than-average volume, suggesting institutional investors are selling. Multiple distribution days in a short window signal market deterioration.
- **VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern):** A chart pattern where price swings get progressively smaller over several weeks, indicating sellers are exhausted and buyers are quietly accumulating. A breakout from a VCP on high volume is a strong buy signal.
- **PEAD:** Post-Earnings Announcement Drift — the tendency for stocks to continue moving in the direction of an earnings surprise for 30-60 days after the report.
- **52-week high:** The highest price a stock/ETF reached in the last 52 weeks. SEC0.DE 52w high = €22.68. Current price €20.76 is 8.5% below it.
- **Stop loss:** A pre-set price level where you automatically exit a position to prevent further losses. SEC0 stop = €18.50 — if SEC0 closes below this for 2 consecutive days, exit the position.
- **RESTRICT posture:** A market posture that means: hold existing positions, but do not open new ones. Only act if a stop is triggered or a new confirmed signal emerges.

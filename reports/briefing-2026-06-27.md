## 10. Glossary

**Distribution Day**: A day when a major index (S&P 500 or NASDAQ) closes down at least 0.2% on higher trading volume than the previous day. Indicates that large institutions (mutual funds, hedge funds) are selling.

**IBD SEVERE**: The highest risk level in the Investor's Business Daily distribution-day framework. Triggered when 7+ distribution days accumulate within 25 sessions and the index trades below its key moving averages. Signals systematic institutional selling.

**21-day EMA / 50-day SMA**: Moving averages — running averages of closing prices over different time periods. Trading below both the 21-day exponential and 50-day simple moving average means the short-term trend has turned bearish.

**VIX (CBOE Volatility Index)**: The "fear gauge." Measures expected market swings over the next 30 days based on options prices. Below 20 = calm market. Above 30 = fear. Above 40 = panic/crisis.

**Uptrend participation (30.4%)**: Of ~2,800 US stocks tracked by Monty's Dashboard, only 30.4% are in a confirmed uptrend. A healthy bull market typically shows 50%+. We are in a weak, narrow market.

**HBM (High Bandwidth Memory)**: Specialized, stacked memory chips used in AI accelerators (NVIDIA GPUs, etc.). Produced by Micron, SK Hynix, Samsung. Key demand driver for the AI capex cycle.

**NVL72 (Rubin platform)**: NVIDIA's next-generation AI server rack system. The rumor is it will use less HBM per rack than previous designs, which would reduce total memory industry demand.

**Dot plot**: The Federal Reserve's quarterly chart where each FOMC member anonymously marks their interest rate forecast for year-end. The June 2026 dot plot median shows 3.8% by December 2026 — implying at least one more 25bp rate hike.

**REDUCE_ONLY**: Exposure coach recommendation meaning: no new equity positions; consider trimming existing holdings on price strength.

**RESTRICT (posture)**: Alessandro's active trading posture — no new buys allowed; hold current positions with firm stops active.

**All-stock deal**: A company acquisition where the buyer pays with its own shares rather than cash. Dilutes existing shareholders. When a company's stock falls on deal announcement, it means the market thinks the buyer overpaid or the deal is poorly timed.

## 10. Glossary

- **VIX:** the market's "fear gauge" — measures how much volatility options traders expect in the S&P 500 over the next 30 days. Below 20 is generally calm; above 25-30 signals real stress.
- **Distribution day:** a day where a major index falls on higher trading volume than the day before — a sign that large institutions are selling, not just retail investors.
- **Composite confidence:** how much this report's overall recommendation should be trusted today, based on how much of the underlying data actually loaded successfully.
- **Exposure ceiling:** the maximum percentage of your portfolio the system thinks should be in risk assets (stocks/ETFs) right now, given current conditions.
- **REDUCE_ONLY:** a posture meaning "don't add new risk; only reduce or hold existing positions."
- **Stop-loss:** a predetermined price level at which you sell a position to limit losses, decided in advance so emotions don't drive the decision in the moment.
- **Breadth:** how many stocks are participating in a market move, versus just a handful of large-cap names — narrow breadth (few stocks driving gains) is historically a warning sign.
- **Uptrend participation:** the percentage of stocks in the market currently in a confirmed uptrend — a broad measure of underlying market health.
- **HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory):** a type of high-performance memory chip used heavily in AI accelerators (like NVIDIA GPUs) — a key growth driver for semiconductor demand.
- **Defensive tilt:** when money rotates out of growth/cyclical sectors (like Technology) into defensive ones (like Healthcare, Utilities) that tend to hold up better in a slowdown.
- **pp (percentage points):** the raw difference between two percentages (e.g., going from 10% to 13% is "+3pp"), not a percentage change.

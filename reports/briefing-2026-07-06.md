## 10. Glossary

- **VIX:** The market's "fear gauge" — measures how much volatility (price swings) traders expect in the S&P 500 over the next 30 days. Below 20 is calm, above 30 signals real stress.
- **Distribution Day:** A day where a major index closes down on higher trading volume than the day before — a sign that large institutions may be selling.
- **Defensive sector rotation:** When money moves out of growth/tech stocks and into "safer" sectors like healthcare, utilities, and consumer staples — often a sign investors are getting cautious.
- **Breadth:** Measures how many stocks are participating in a market move, not just the big index names. Narrow breadth (few stocks driving gains) is considered less healthy than broad participation.
- **pp (percentage points):** The plain difference between two percentages (e.g., 32.3% minus 24.4% = 7.9pp) — not a percentage change.
- **HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory):** A specialized, high-speed memory chip used heavily in AI accelerators (like NVIDIA GPUs) — currently a key supply bottleneck in the AI chip industry.
- **Stop loss:** A predetermined price at which you exit a losing position to limit further loss — for SEC0, this is €18.50 (needs 2 consecutive closes below to trigger).
- **Composite confidence:** This report's overall reliability score, based on how many of the underlying data tools worked and how well they agree with each other.

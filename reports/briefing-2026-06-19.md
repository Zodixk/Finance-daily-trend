## 11. Glossary

- **Juneteenth:** US federal holiday on June 19. NYSE and NASDAQ are closed. European exchanges remain open.
- **FOMC:** Federal Open Market Committee — the Fed committee that sets US interest rates. Meets 8 times a year.
- **Dot plot:** A chart the Fed releases at each meeting showing where each of the 18 members expects interest rates to be in future years. When the median dot shifts higher, it means the Fed is leaning toward higher rates.
- **Hawkish:** When the Fed signals it wants to raise rates or keep them high to fight inflation. The opposite is "dovish" (lower rates to stimulate growth).
- **Rate hike:** When the Fed increases interest rates. This makes borrowing more expensive and tends to reduce valuations of growth stocks (like tech) because future earnings are discounted more heavily.
- **SOXX:** iShares Semiconductor ETF — the most-watched US semiconductor sector fund. Used as a proxy for the health of the global chip sector. Not the same as SEC0.DE (your ETF), but closely correlated.
- **VIX:** The "fear index" — measures expected volatility in the S&P 500. Below 20 = calm; 20-30 = elevated fear; above 30 = high stress; above 35 triggers the CB1 circuit breaker in your routine.
- **Uptrend ratio:** Percentage of roughly 2,800 US stocks currently in an uptrend. 27.6% means fewer than 1 in 3 stocks is trending up — a relatively low reading, consistent with a narrower rally.
- **BROADENING regime:** A market phase where returns are spreading from mega-cap tech to smaller companies and other sectors. Historically healthy — but a transition out of broadening can signal a slowdown.
- **Transition probability:** The macro regime detector's estimate of how likely the current regime (Broadening) is to shift to a different regime soon. 70-90% means it's likely but not yet confirmed.
- **PCE:** Personal Consumption Expenditures price index — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge (more comprehensive than CPI). Released monthly, this is what the Fed watches when deciding on rate hikes.
- **Dead-cat bounce:** A temporary recovery in a declining asset that does not indicate a genuine trend reversal. Named because "even a dead cat bounces if dropped from high enough."
- **VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern):** A chart pattern where a stock or ETF moves sideways for weeks with progressively smaller price swings — signals that sellers are exhausted and a breakout may be near. One of your SEC0 re-entry triggers.
- **SEMI.AS:** Amsterdam Stock Exchange listing of the same iShares MSCI Global Semiconductors ETF (same fund as SEC0.DE on XETRA). Prices may differ slightly due to FX and exchange timing.
- **Distribution phase:** When a large institutional investor (fund, bank) quietly sells a position over days or weeks while the price holds steady or rises slightly on lower volume. The opposite of accumulation. A sign that "smart money" is exiting.

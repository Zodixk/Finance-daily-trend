## 10. Glossary

- **SOXX**: iShares Semiconductor ETF — a US-listed ETF tracking the 30 largest US semiconductor companies (NVDA, AVGO, ASML, MU, etc.). When SOXX falls sharply, SEC0 (which tracks global semis) typically follows closely.
- **HBM / HBM4**: High Bandwidth Memory — specialized AI chip memory. HBM4 is the newest generation. Only three companies make it: SK Hynix (Korea), Micron (US), Samsung (Korea). It goes inside NVIDIA's GPU chips. If HBM demand slows, Micron and SK Hynix suffer most.
- **KOSPI**: South Korea's main stock index. Heavily weighted to Samsung and SK Hynix. A -9.99% drop with circuit breakers means extreme fear — last seen at this scale in the 2020 COVID crash.
- **Circuit breaker**: An automatic market pause when a stock index falls too fast (Korea triggers at -8%). Prevents a cascade from becoming a full crash. When it fires, it signals genuine market shock.
- **PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures)**: The Federal Reserve's preferred inflation measure, released monthly by the US Bureau of Economic Analysis. Higher than expected → more rate hikes likely → growth stocks fall.
- **Rate hike**: The Fed raises the interest rate it charges banks. Result: borrowing becomes more expensive, the economy slows, and high-growth stocks (like semiconductors) fall because their future profits are worth less in today's money (higher discount rate).
- **Hard stop / two-consecutive-close rule**: Your pre-set exit rule for SEC0.DE. If SEC0 closes below €18.50 for two consecutive trading days → sell. One close below is not enough — you need confirmation to avoid selling on a temporary spike down.
- **CB2 (Circuit Breaker 2)**: An internal quality rule that caps today's confidence at 45% because more than 3 data tools failed to return data. It means the analysis relies on partial data — act with extra caution.
- **Composite confidence 45%**: Today's overall reliability score for this briefing. Low because many automated data tools were blocked by proxy or subscription restrictions. Core FMP (US quotes) and web search were available; EU ETF prices, market breadth, and sector scripts were not.

## 10. Glossary

*Every technical term used today, explained in plain language:*

- **VIX** — The "fear gauge." Measures how nervous professional investors are about the near future. Below 20 = calm, 20-30 = moderate concern, above 30 = panic. Today: 17.53 = calm.
- **Breadth / Uptrend participation** — The percentage of individual stocks that are currently rising. If only 20% of stocks are rising while an index goes up, a handful of giants are pulling the average. If 60%+ are rising, the rally is genuine and healthy. Today: 31.5%, up from 30.4%.
- **8/11 sectors uptrending** — The S&P 500 is divided into 11 industry groups (Technology, Healthcare, Energy, etc.). Today, 8 of them are collectively in a rising trend. On Jun 26, only 6 were. More sectors uptrending = healthier market.
- **RESTRICT posture** — Hold existing positions, do not add new ones, do not average down on losers. Protects capital while the environment is uncertain.
- **Distribution day** — A day when a major index falls on higher trading volume than the day before. It means institutional investors (funds, banks) are selling. When you accumulate 6-7+ in a few weeks, it's a warning sign that smart money is exiting. Estimated current: 6-7.
- **PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures)** — The Federal Reserve's preferred inflation measure. At 4.1%, it's more than double the Fed's 2% target. Higher PCE = Fed keeps interest rates high = growth stocks are under pressure.
- **NFP (Non-Farm Payrolls)** — Monthly US government report counting new jobs created. Strong jobs = economy overheating = Fed raises rates more = bad for growth stocks. Weak jobs = economy cooling = Fed may pause = good for stocks (temporarily).
- **ISM Manufacturing PMI** — Monthly survey of factory purchasing managers. Above 50 = factories are ordering more materials and hiring (expansion). Below 50 = contraction. Semiconductors power factories, so this directly affects chip demand.
- **Conference Board Consumer Confidence** — How confident US households feel about the economy and their personal finances. High confidence = they'll keep spending = economy stays healthy.
- **VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern)** — A chart pattern where a stock moves sideways for several weeks with increasingly smaller price swings. Signals that sellers are exhausted. Safe to add a position when this pattern completes after a breakout above the consolidation range. SEC0 needs approximately 3.3 more weeks to complete a 5-week VCP base.
- **Hard stop** — A firm price level below which you automatically sell to limit losses. SEC0's hard stop is €18.50. The rule: exit if it closes below this for 2 consecutive sessions (two-consecutive-close rule protects against false breaks).
- **Onshoring** — Moving manufacturing back to your home country. The US and EU are spending billions to build domestic chip factories, reducing dependency on Taiwan and Asia. Creates a government-subsidized demand floor for semiconductor companies.
- **AI capex** — Capital spending by large tech companies (Google, Meta, Microsoft, Amazon) and governments on AI computing infrastructure — data centers, servers, networking. More AI capex = more chips ordered = SEC0 thesis validated.
- **CB2 (Circuit Breaker 2)** — Internal risk rule: if 3 or more data sources fail in one session, cap confidence at 55% to prevent overconfident decisions from incomplete data. Active today.
- **PEAD (Post-Earnings Drift)** — The tendency of stocks to keep moving in the direction of an earnings surprise for 30-60 days after the report. After TSMC Q2 earnings on Jul 16, this pattern may apply if TSMC beats estimates.
- **Composite confidence** — A weighted average of how reliable all the signals are today, considering which data tools succeeded, which failed, and how consistent the signals are with each other. Today: 48% (below minimum 50% threshold — caution warranted).

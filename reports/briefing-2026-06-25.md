## 10. Glossary

- **PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures):** The Fed's preferred inflation measure — tracks how much Americans spend on goods and services. Higher PCE = more inflation = Fed may raise interest rates, which hurts growth stocks.
- **HBM (High Bandwidth Memory):** A special, very fast memory chip used in AI accelerators like NVIDIA's GPUs. Much more expensive than normal RAM. Micron, SK Hynix, and Samsung are the three main producers. "Sold out" means demand exceeds supply — a strong pricing signal.
- **EPS (Earnings Per Share):** A company's net profit divided by the number of shares outstanding. If EPS "beats" the estimate, the company earned more than analysts expected — usually positive for the stock price.
- **Guidance:** A company's own forecast for future revenue or profit. Often more market-moving than actual past results, because it tells you where the business is going, not where it's been.
- **Hard stop:** A pre-set price at which you exit a position to limit losses. Alessandro's hard stop for SEC0 is €18.50 — applies if SEC0 closes below that level for 2 consecutive days.
- **Two-consecutive-close rule:** Alessandro's rule to avoid panic-selling on a single volatile day. SEC0 must close BELOW €18.50 on TWO separate consecutive days before exiting. Prevents exits on temporary dips.
- **VIX:** The "fear index" — measures how volatile options traders expect the US market to be. Below 20 = calm/risk-on. 20-30 = elevated. Above 30 = fear/risk-off. Currently 18.01 and falling — positive signal.
- **CB2 (Circuit Breaker 2):** A safety rule in this briefing: if more than 3 data sources fail, overall confidence is capped at 45% to prevent overconfident decisions with incomplete data.
- **Composite confidence:** A score (0-100%) combining all signal quality and consistency. Capped at 45% today due to CB2.
- **RESTRICT posture:** This briefing's recommendation to limit new investments. Hold existing positions, no new purchases, wait for clearer signals.
- **Re-entry trigger:** A specific, pre-defined condition that must be met before adding more money to SEC0. Three triggers defined: price down ≥25% from high (≤€17.54), VIX above 25, or 5 consecutive weeks of sideways base formation. None are currently active.

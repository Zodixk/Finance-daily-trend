## 10. Glossary

- **VIX:** the "fear index" — measures how much volatility options traders expect in the S&P 500 over the next 30 days. Higher = more nervous market.
- **Uptrend participation / breadth:** what percentage of stocks (not just the big ones) are actually in an uptrend. Low breadth with a stable index means only a few large stocks are propping up the market.
- **Distribution Day:** a day where a major index closes lower on higher trading volume than the day before — a sign that large institutional investors are selling.
- **Market Top Detector:** a composite score estimating the probability of a 10-20% correction in the next 2-8 weeks, based on distribution days, leading-stock health, and sector rotation.
- **Exposure ceiling:** the maximum % of your portfolio the routine recommends having invested in stocks/ETFs right now, based on combined risk signals.
- **Sector rotation:** money moving from one sector (e.g., Technology) into another (e.g., Healthcare) as investor risk appetite changes.
- **Defensive sectors:** sectors like Healthcare, Utilities, and Consumer Staples that tend to hold up better when investors get nervous, because demand for their products doesn't depend much on the economic cycle.
- **Hard stop:** a predetermined price level where, if breached (per your rule, for 2 consecutive closes), you exit the position regardless of your longer-term view, to limit losses.
- **Composite confidence:** a weighted score reflecting how much the day's overall reading can be trusted, reduced when data sources fail or signals conflict.

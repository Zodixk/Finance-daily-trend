## 10. Glossary

**VIX:** The CBOE Volatility Index. Measures how much the market expects the S&P 500 to move in the next 30 days. Below 20 = calm. Above 30 = fear. Above 40 = panic.

**VWCE.DE:** Vanguard FTSE All-World UCITS ETF (accumulating). Your core holding. Tracks ~4,000 stocks from 50+ countries, ~60% US. Grows by reinvesting dividends automatically — no tax until you sell.

**SEC0.DE:** iShares MSCI Global Semiconductors UCITS ETF. Your satellite position. Tracks ~261 semiconductor companies worldwide. Top holdings: NVDA, TSMC, ASML, AVGO, Micron. ISIN: IE000I8KRLL9.

**S&P 500:** An index of 500 of the largest US companies. Used as a proxy for the overall US stock market. When MRVL "joins the S&P 500," every fund that tracks this index must buy the stock.

**NASDAQ:** A stock exchange and index focused on technology companies. More volatile than the S&P 500, heavily weighted toward tech and semiconductors.

**PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures):** The Federal Reserve's preferred measure of inflation. Tracks what consumers are actually paying for goods and services. Higher = more inflation = more pressure to raise rates.

**FOMC (Federal Open Market Committee):** The committee within the Federal Reserve that sets interest rates. Meets roughly every 6–8 weeks.

**Dot Plot:** A chart published by the Fed after each FOMC meeting showing where each official expects interest rates to be in future years. A "flipped dot plot" means the median official now expects rates to go UP instead of down — a hawkish shift.

**Rate hike:** When the Federal Reserve raises interest rates. Makes borrowing more expensive, reduces corporate profit multiples, and historically hurts growth stocks (tech, semiconductors).

**SOXX:** iShares Semiconductor ETF (US-listed). Tracks the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index. A proxy for the health of the global semiconductor sector. Currently ~78.86% above its 200-day average — an extreme overextension.

**HBM (High Bandwidth Memory):** A type of ultra-fast memory chip used in AI data centers (for training AI models). Micron, SK Hynix, and Samsung are the primary manufacturers. HBM demand has exploded due to AI — Micron's HBM capacity is fully booked through 2026.

**AI Capex:** Capital expenditure on AI infrastructure by hyperscalers (Google, Microsoft, Meta, Amazon). When these companies spend $725B on AI in 2026, most of that money flows to semiconductor suppliers.

**VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern):** A chart pattern where a stock's or ETF's price range gets progressively smaller over several weeks. Signals that sellers are exhausted and a new breakout may be coming. One of your three re-entry triggers for adding to SEC0.

**Soft stop loss:** An informal warning threshold (-7 to -8% from entry). When reached, you stop adding and monitor closely. Not an automatic sell — it is a caution zone. SEC0 is at this threshold today.

**Hard stop loss:** A definite exit price. If SEC0 closes below €18.50 for 2 consecutive days, you exit the position entirely. No exceptions — this protects you from a catastrophic loss.

**Macro regime:** The overall "phase" the economy and stock market are in — Concentration (narrow leadership), Broadening (more stocks participating), or Contraction (bear market). Current regime: Broadening (transition probability: 70–90%).

**Dot-com bubble:** The technology stock bubble of 1999–2000. When it burst, NASDAQ fell 78% from peak to trough. Michael Burry is comparing today's semiconductor rally to the final months before that peak in June 2000.

**MRVL / Marvell Technology:** A US semiconductor company specializing in custom AI chips and networking silicon. Joined the S&P 500 on June 22, 2026. Up 210% YTD. Strategic partner of NVIDIA for AI infrastructure.

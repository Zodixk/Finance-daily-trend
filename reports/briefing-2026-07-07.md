## 10. Glossary

- **VIX:** the "fear index" — measures how much volatility options traders expect in the S&P 500 over the next 30 days. Below 20 = calm; above 30 = panic.
- **FOMC minutes:** the detailed written record of a Federal Reserve policy meeting, released a few weeks after the meeting itself — often contains more nuance than the initial statement.
- **Dot plot:** a chart where each Fed policymaker anonymously marks where they expect interest rates to be — the median dot is the market's best guess at future Fed policy.
- **Sector rotation:** when investors move money from one group of stocks (e.g., tech) into another (e.g., healthcare/financials), usually reflecting a change in risk appetite.
- **Uptrend participation rate:** the % of stocks in the market currently in a technical uptrend. Higher = broader, healthier rally.
- **Exposure ceiling:** the maximum % of a portfolio that a model recommends keeping invested in risky assets (stocks/ETFs) right now, given current conditions.
- **Composite confidence:** how much trust to place in today's overall read, based on how much of the underlying data actually loaded successfully and how consistent the signals were with each other.
- **Stop loss / two-consecutive-close rule:** a predetermined price where you exit a losing position to cap further losses; here, the rule requires two consecutive daily closes below the stop level (not just one intraday dip) before triggering an exit.
- **ETF:** Exchange-Traded Fund — a basket of stocks (like SEC0 or VWCE) that trades like a single stock.

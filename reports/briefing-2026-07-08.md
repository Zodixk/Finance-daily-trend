## 10. Glossary

- **VIX:** the "fear index" — measures how much volatility options traders expect in the S&P 500 over the next 30 days. Higher = more fear/uncertainty.
- **Uptrend participation / ratio:** the percentage of stocks in the market currently in a confirmed uptrend. Higher = more stocks rising together (healthy); lower = only a few stocks carrying the market (fragile).
- **Sector rotation:** when investors move money from one industry group (e.g., Technology) into another (e.g., Healthcare), usually signaling a change in risk appetite.
- **Defensive Tilt:** a market condition where "safe" sectors (Healthcare, Utilities, Consumer Staples) are outperforming "risky" sectors (Technology, Consumer Discretionary) — usually a sign investors are getting cautious.
- **Distribution day:** a day where a major index falls on higher trading volume than the previous day, suggesting large institutional investors ("smart money") are selling.
- **Composite confidence:** how much Alessandro's daily "report card" should be trusted, based on how much of the underlying data actually loaded successfully today.
- **Circuit Breaker (CB2):** an automatic safety rule in this routine — if more than 3 of the underlying data tools fail, the report's confidence score is automatically capped, so the routine doesn't overstate certainty when data is missing.
- **Exposure ceiling:** the maximum percentage of the portfolio that should be in risk assets (stocks/ETFs) right now, based on current market health.
- **REDUCE_ONLY / RESTRICT:** a posture meaning "don't add new risk — only reduce or hold existing positions" until conditions improve.
- **Stop loss:** a pre-set price level at which a position is sold automatically (or manually) to limit further losses.
- **FOMC minutes:** the detailed written record of the Federal Reserve's most recent interest-rate meeting, released about 3 weeks after the meeting — gives more color than the initial rate-decision statement.
- **50-day moving average:** the average closing price of an asset over the last 50 trading days — a common technical support/resistance level; falling below it is often read as a bearish signal.

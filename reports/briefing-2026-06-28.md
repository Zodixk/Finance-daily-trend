## 10. Glossary

**VIX (Volatility Index):** The "fear gauge" — measures how much the market expects prices to swing in the next 30 days. Below 20 = calm. Above 30 = fear. Above 35 = crisis.

**RESTRICT posture:** You are not allowed to open new positions. Existing positions stay unless stops are hit. Yellow light — slow down, don't accelerate.

**IBD SEVERE:** IBD (Investor's Business Daily) classification for when there are 6+ days of heavy institutional selling in the last 25 trading sessions. Institutions are net sellers.

**Distribution Day:** A day when the market closes down at least 0.2% on higher volume than the day before. Higher volume + lower prices = institutions are selling to retail investors.

**PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures):** The Fed's preferred inflation measure. When PCE is high, the Fed may raise interest rates to cool the economy, which typically hurts growth stocks.

**HBM4 (High Bandwidth Memory 4):** The latest generation of ultra-fast memory used in AI processors (like NVIDIA's GPUs). "Sold out" means demand for AI chips is far ahead of supply — a structural positive.

**VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern):** A chart pattern where a stock or ETF moves sideways with progressively smaller price swings over 5+ weeks. Signals sellers exhausted — one of SEC0's re-entry triggers.

**ISM Manufacturing PMI:** Monthly survey of factory managers. Above 50 = factories are expanding. Below 50 = contraction. Important for semiconductor demand (chips go into manufactured goods).

**NFP (Non-Farm Payrolls):** Monthly US jobs report — how many jobs were added outside farming. The most market-moving data release. Strong jobs = Fed may hike. Weak jobs = Fed holds. Released first Friday of each month.

**21-day EMA / 50-day SMA:** Rolling averages of stock prices. When SPY trades below both, it means the trend is pointing down in both the short and medium term — a warning signal.

**SOCAMM memory:** A specific type of memory module used in NVIDIA's Vera Rubin AI servers. NVIDIA cut the capacity of these modules per rack, reducing demand for DRAM memory from Micron and others.

**SOXX:** The iShares Semiconductor ETF — a US-listed ETF tracking semiconductor stocks. Often moves similarly to your SEC0.DE (which tracks the same sector but is listed in EUR on XETRA).

**CB2 (Circuit Breaker 2):** A safety rule in this briefing routine — if 3+ data sources fail, composite confidence is automatically capped at 55% to avoid overconfidence in incomplete data.

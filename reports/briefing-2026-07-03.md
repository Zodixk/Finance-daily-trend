## 10. Glossary

- **VIX:** the "fear index" — measures how much volatility options traders expect in the S&P 500 over the next 30 days. Below 20 = calm; above 30 = panic.
- **NFP (Nonfarm Payrolls):** the monthly US jobs report — how many jobs the economy added, excluding farm workers. A key signal for Fed policy.
- **FOMC:** the Federal Reserve committee that sets US interest rates.
- **Distribution day:** a day where a major index (like the S&P 500) closes down on higher trading volume than the day before — a sign of institutional selling. Many of these clustered together = "SEVERE" risk.
- **Sector rotation:** when investors move money from one group of stocks (e.g., tech) into another (e.g., healthcare/utilities), usually reflecting a change in risk appetite.
- **Defensive tilt:** when money is flowing into sectors that hold up better in a downturn (healthcare, utilities, consumer staples) rather than growth sectors (tech) — a caution signal.
- **Uptrend participation rate:** the % of stocks in the market that are currently in a technical uptrend. Higher = broader, healthier rally.
- **Composite confidence:** how much trust to place in today's overall read, based on how much of the underlying data actually loaded successfully and how consistent the signals were with each other.
- **Stop loss:** a predetermined price at which you exit a losing position to cap further losses.
- **ETF:** Exchange-Traded Fund — a basket of stocks (like SEC0 or VWCE) that trades like a single stock.

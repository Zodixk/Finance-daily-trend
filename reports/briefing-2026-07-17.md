## 10. Glossary

- **VIX**: the market's "fear gauge" — derived from S&P 500 options prices, it estimates how much volatility traders expect over the next 30 days. Higher = more fear/uncertainty priced in.
- **Distribution day**: a session where a major index closes down on higher volume than the day before — a sign that large institutional investors are selling. Several in a short window (like SPY's 2/4/8 today) signal that big money may be exiting.
- **Basis points / pp (percentage points)**: a direct difference between two percentages (e.g., 27.6% minus 23.6% = 3.9pp), not a percentage change.
- **Uptrend ratio / breadth**: the percentage of ~2,800 tracked US stocks currently in a confirmed uptrend. Low and falling = few stocks participating in any rally, a fragile market even if headline indices look fine.
- **Sector rotation**: the tendency of money to flow from one group of sectors (e.g., cyclical/growth) to another (e.g., defensive) as investors reposition for a changing economic outlook.
- **Capex (capital expenditure)**: money a company spends building or upgrading physical assets — for a chipmaker like TSMC, this means new factories ("fabs") and equipment. Higher capex now can mean lower near-term profit margins even if it funds future growth.
- **Stop-loss**: a predetermined price level at which you exit a position to limit losses, regardless of your longer-term view on the asset.
- **Exposure ceiling**: the maximum percentage of your investable capital that current market conditions justify having in risk assets (stocks/ETFs) at any one time.
- **Composite confidence**: how much trust to place in today's overall analysis, based on how much of the underlying data actually loaded successfully. Capped lower when several data sources fail, even if the data that did load looks clear.

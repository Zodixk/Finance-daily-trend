## 10. Glossary

**VIX:** The "Fear Index" — measures how nervous options traders are about the S&P 500. Below 20 = calm. 20–30 = elevated concern. Above 30 = fear. Above 40 = panic. Today's reading of ~17–20 is elevated but not alarming.

**Hard stop:** A predetermined price level where you sell automatically, no questions asked. For SEC0, this is €18.50. The rule: if SEC0 CLOSES below €18.50 for 2 consecutive trading days → exit. No averaging down, no waiting.

**PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures):** The Federal Reserve's preferred inflation measure. A "hot" print (high inflation) makes rate cuts less likely and rate hikes more likely — bad for growth stocks.

**Dot plot:** A chart the Federal Reserve publishes showing where each official expects interest rates to go. A "hawkish dot plot" means officials expect rates to stay high or go higher. The June 17 update was hawkish.

**Exposure ceiling:** The maximum percentage of your portfolio that should be in stocks given current market conditions. Today: 54%.

**RESTRICT posture:** No new stock positions. Hold existing positions. If prices bounce, consider trimming (selling a small amount). Opposite of ALLOW.

**HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory):** Advanced memory chips used in AI processors like NVIDIA's GPUs. Micron's HBM capacity is sold out for all of 2026 — every chip they make is already pre-committed to a buyer.

**Uptrend ratio:** The percentage of stocks in a sector that are currently in an uptrend. Technology at 33.7% means roughly 1 in 3 tech stocks is trending up right now.

**Cyclical vs. Defensive sectors:** Cyclical sectors (Tech, Consumer Discretionary) do better when the economy grows. Defensive sectors (Utilities, Healthcare) hold up better in slowdowns. When cyclicals lead defensives by 8.7pp (as today), it is a "risk-on" breadth signal.

**SpaceX IPO liquidity drain:** When SpaceX went public (June 12, 2026), institutions needed ~$75 billion in cash to subscribe to the IPO. They raised this by selling existing tech holdings. This mechanical selling (not based on fundamentals) has been depressing the tech sector for ~2 weeks post-IPO.

**Pre-earnings run-up:** The tendency for stocks to rise in the 1–3 weeks before a major earnings report as investors position for a beat. MU hit all-time highs ahead of June 24 — meaning a strong beat may already be partially priced in.

## Glossary

| Term | Plain English explanation |
|------|--------------------------|
| **VIX** | The "Fear Index." Below 20 = calm market. Above 30 = panic. Today's 16.78 = investors are relaxed. |
| **PCE** | Personal Consumption Expenditures — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge. High PCE = inflation still hot = rate hike more likely. |
| **Dot plot** | A chart published after each Fed meeting showing where each of the 18 FOMC officials expects interest rates to be. When dots shift up, rates are expected to rise. |
| **FOMC** | Federal Open Market Committee — the group inside the Federal Reserve that votes on interest rates. Meets 8 times per year. |
| **Hawkish** | When a central bank signals rates may go UP or stay high longer. Opposite: dovish (rates coming down). Hawkish = negative for growth stocks in the short term. |
| **HBM (High Bandwidth Memory)** | Advanced memory chips used inside AI systems like NVIDIA GPUs. Micron, SK Hynix, and Samsung are the main suppliers. Currently in very short supply. |
| **Uptrend ratio** | The percentage of ~2,800 US stocks currently in an uptrend. 27.7% means only 1 in 4 stocks is rising — below the historical average. |
| **Breadth** | How many stocks are participating in a market move. Low breadth = a few large companies driving the index. High breadth = the whole market is rising together. |
| **S&P 500 inclusion (MRVL)** | When a company joins the S&P 500 index, every ETF tracking the index must buy its shares — creating forced demand. This usually supports the price around the inclusion date. |
| **Distribution day** | A day when a major index falls on higher-than-usual trading volume. Multiple distribution days = large investors (institutions) are selling. A warning sign. |
| **Composite confidence** | How reliable today's analysis is, expressed as a %. 45% = low reliability (missing key data). Signals are directionally correct but keep position sizes conservative. |
| **SOXX** | The iShares US Semiconductor ETF — tracks the largest US chip stocks. Similar to SEC0 but US-only. Used here as a real-time proxy for the semiconductor sector. |
| **Regime (Broadening)** | A market phase where returns spread beyond mega-cap tech — mid-cap, value, and international stocks start to participate. Healthy diversification signal. |
